# Flowcus XP System Redesign — Research & Plan

## Part 1: Research Synthesis

### The ADHD Reward Brain — What Science Says

#### 1. Dopamine Reward Pathway is Structurally Different

PET imaging studies (Volkow et al.) found that ADHD brains have lower D2/D3 receptor and dopamine transporter availability in the nucleus accumbens and midbrain — the core reward/motivation regions. This isn't just "attention deficit" — it's a **motivation deficit**. The brain literally produces less dopamine and has fewer receptors to process it. Completing a task that would give a neurotypical brain a satisfying "done" feeling often produces a **frustratingly neutral feeling** for the ADHD brain.

**Sources:**
- [Motivation Deficit in ADHD and Dopamine Reward Pathway (Volkow et al.)](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3010326/)
- [Evaluating Dopamine Reward Pathway in ADHD — JAMA](https://jamanetwork.com/journals/jama/fullarticle/184547)
- [Deficits in Brain's Reward System Observed in ADHD Patients](https://www.bnl.gov/newsroom/news.php?a=110998)

#### 2. Immediate Over Delayed — It's Neurological, Not Laziness

Meta-analyses confirm ADHD individuals consistently choose small immediate rewards over larger delayed ones. Lower baseline dopamine means an immediate reward provides a much-needed neurochemical boost, while a future reward doesn't stimulate the brain *now*. This has a direct design implication: **XP that accumulates silently toward nothing tangible is almost invisible to the ADHD brain.**

**Sources:**
- [Delay Discounting of Reward in ADHD](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3059765/)
- [ADHD: Small Immediate Over Larger Delayed Rewards — Meta-Analysis](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/abs/10.1177/1087054718772138)
- [Association of Neurocircuitry with Deficit of Delayed Gratification in ADHD](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6305413/)

#### 3. Variable Ratio Reinforcement = Maximum Dopamine

Research shows that variable ratio reinforcement schedules (unpredictable reward timing/amount) produce **greater dopamine release** in the Nucleus Accumbens than fixed ratio schedules — even when the actual behavioral outcomes are identical. The unpredictability *itself* drives enhanced dopamine sensitivity. This is the mechanism behind why games are engaging. Fixed "+25 XP every time" is the weakest possible reinforcement schedule.

**Sources:**
- [How Uncertainty Sensitizes Dopamine Neurons](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6300523/)
- [Reinforcement Schedule and Experience-Dependent Motivation Changes](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9090977/)
- [Reinforcement Schedules — Simply Psychology](https://www.simplypsychology.org/schedules-of-reinforcement.html)

#### 4. Extrinsic Rewards Don't Crowd Out Intrinsic Motivation in ADHD

For neurotypical individuals, external rewards can reduce internal motivation (the "overjustification effect"). But research shows ADHD individuals score highest on amotivation and extrinsic motivation, lowest on intrinsic. Studies confirm that under continuous positive reinforcement, **ADHD children perform comparably to neurotypical children** — the gap closes. Strategic extrinsic rewards actually *enhance* performance for ADHD. This means: **lean into the reward system, don't hold back.**

**Sources:**
- [Studying Motivation in ADHD: Self-Determination Theory](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9066661/)
- [Impact of Motivation on Attention in Children with ADHD](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8238702/)
- [Motivation Crowding Out Theory and ADHD](https://www.focusbear.io/blog-post/how-motivation-crowding-out-theory-adhd-affects-you)

#### 5. Novelty Seeking + Fast Habituation = Systems Go Stale

ADHD brains respond more strongly to novelty's dopamine surge but habituate faster. Once the "shiny new thing" effect wears off, motivation drops sharply. A static XP counter that never changes how it works becomes invisible within weeks. The system needs **rotation, surprise, and variety** to maintain engagement.

**Sources:**
- [Neurocomputational Account of Reward and Novelty in ADHD](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC5917772/)
- [ADHD Novelty Seeking and Dopamine](https://www.addept.org/living-with-adult-add-adhd/adhd-novelty-motivation-focus)
- [The Allure of Novelty for Those with ADHD](https://www.theminiadhdcoach.com/living-with-adhd/adhd-novelty)

#### 6. Shame is the #1 Enemy

Dr. Hallowell calls shame "the single most debilitating part of having ADHD." Shame spirals — where a perceived failure cascades into avoidance, which causes more failure — are the primary mechanism of app abandonment. Traditional streak mechanics (Day 1, Day 2... broken... Day 0) are a **shame spiral trigger**. Interestingly, research also shows ADHD individuals display *less* loss aversion, meaning punitive mechanics (losing progress) are both harmful AND ineffective.

**Sources:**
- [Loss Aversion and ADHD Symptomatology](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s44202-023-00104-3)
- [ADHD Shame Spirals — Psychology Today](https://www.psychologytoday.com/us/blog/promoting-empathy-with-your-teen/202504/the-adhd-shame-cycle-always-feeling-behind)
- [Shame: The Most Debilitating Part of ADHD — ADDitude](https://www.additudemag.com/slideshows/overcoming-adhd-and-shame/)
- [ADHD and Shame — Simply Psychology](https://www.simplypsychology.org/adhd-and-shame.html)

#### 7. Self-Determination Theory: Three Needs Are Unmet

Research shows ADHD individuals report significantly lower satisfaction AND higher frustration across all three basic psychological needs:
- **Autonomy** — feeling in control of choices
- **Competence** — feeling capable and seeing growth
- **Relatedness** — feeling connected

A reward system should nurture all three.

**Sources:**
- [SDT: Self-Regulation in ADHD](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/36548057/)
- [ADHD and Psychological Need Fulfillment](https://selfdeterminationtheory.org/wp-content/uploads/2023/03/InPress_SerranoAbu-RamadanEtAl_ADHD.pdf)
- [Designing for Sustained Motivation — SDT in Behaviour Change Technologies](https://academic.oup.com/iwc/advance-article/doi/10.1093/iwc/iwae040/7760010)

#### 8. Micro-Rewards and Immediate Feedback

ADHD brains don't get the natural dopamine hit from task completion that neurotypicals do. External celebration/feedback MUST fill this gap. Physical/sensory feedback (haptics, animation) creates stronger memory traces. Points per step + progress bars + visual cues = continuous encouragement loop. Adults with ADHD symptoms are more responsive to immediate feedback stimuli.

**Sources:**
- [Rewards Enhance Stimulus Processing in ADHD — Neurophysiology](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3807761/)
- [Closing the Dopamine Gap: Celebrating Wins with ADHD](https://www.addrc.org/closing-the-dopamine-gap-how-to-actually-celebrate-wins-with-adhd/)
- [Feedback Timing Modulates Probabilistic Learning in Adults with ADHD](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-018-33551-3)
- [Carrots vs. Sticks: Reward and Punishment for ADHD](https://www.additudemag.com/positive-reinforcement-reward-and-punishment-adhd/)

#### 9. Gamification App Effectiveness — Hard Data

An 8-week randomized trial showed gamified educational interventions significantly enhanced attention and academic performance in ADHD children vs non-gamified programs. Apps with game elements had 48% higher retention. A study with adolescents reported a 60% boost in compliance with points, badges, and streaks.

**Sources:**
- [Gamified ADHD App: 8-Week Randomized Trial](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/education/articles/10.3389/feduc.2025.1668260/full)
- [Effectiveness of Serious Games for ADHD — JMIR 2025](https://games.jmir.org/2025/1/e60937)
- [Gamification in ADHD Apps — Retention Data](https://imaginovation.net/blog/gamification-adhd-apps-user-retention/)
- [Mobile Game Prototype for ADHD — Feasibility Testing](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11164876/)

## Part 3: The New System — "Momentum & Milestones"

### Core Design Principles

1. **Every reward is FELT** — animation, haptics, visible number going up
2. **Variable ratio bonuses** — unpredictable extras alongside predictable base
3. **No shame, ever** — momentum decays gently, never "breaks"
4. **Novelty through variety** — different reward types, surprise milestones, rotating celebrations
5. **Progress toward tangible goals** — XP feeds a level system with visual progress
6. **Competence over time** — show growth patterns, not just numbers
7. **Reward breaks too** — taking breaks is part of ADHD self-regulation skill

---

### A. Level System (Replaces Raw XP Counter)

XP feeds into named levels with a clear progress bar. The curve is gentle and encouraging:

| Level | Name | Total XP | XP to Next |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Spark | 0 | 100 |
| 2 | Kindling | 100 | 150 |
| 3 | Flame | 250 | 200 |
| 4 | Blaze | 450 | 250 |
| 5 | Furnace | 700 | 300 |
| 6 | Inferno | 1000 | 400 |
| 7+ | ... | continues | +50 per level |

Level names use a fire/energy metaphor that ties into the existing wave/liquid visual language. The progress bar is visible on the Focus tab when idle (not during a session — no distraction).

**Why this works for ADHD:** Turns abstract XP into a concrete, visual "filling up" sensation. The endowed progress effect (starting at Level 1 "Spark", not Level 0) gives immediate competence feeling. Gentle curve means early levels come fast = quick dopamine.

---

### B. Reward Moments (Replaces Silent XP Addition)

After each session completes, BEFORE the mood prompt, show a **Reward Card** — a brief animated overlay.

**Base Rewards (predictable — satisfies need for structure):**
- Focus session: 25 XP
- Short break completed: 5 XP *(new — rewards self-regulation)*
- Long break completed: 10 XP *(new)*

**Variable Bonuses (unpredictable — maximum dopamine):**
- **"Flow State"** — completed session without pausing: +10 XP (~always when earned, but behavior is variable)
- **"Critical Focus"** — random ~20% chance on any focus session: 2x base XP (so +25 extra)
- **"Momentum Boost"** — scales with momentum tier: +3/6/9/12/15 XP
- **"Daily Compound"** — 2nd session today +5, 3rd +10, 4th +15, etc. (stacking same-day bonus)

**Runway Bonuses (kept, now visible on the Reward Card):**
- Tier cleared: Small +10, Medium +25, Big +50
- Full runway cleared: +100

The Reward Card shows each line item appearing one by one with a satisfying animation (number ticking up, haptic per line). Total XP earned this session shown prominently. If a level-up happens, a special celebration triggers.

**Why this works for ADHD:** Immediate, sensory, visible. The variable bonuses create unpredictability (variable ratio). The line-item reveal creates anticipation. The "Critical Focus" random roll adds a game-like excitement that sustains novelty.

---

### C. Momentum (Replaces Streak)

Replace the concept of "streak" entirely. Momentum is a 1-5 tier system that **grows with consistency but decays slowly, never resets to zero.**

| Tier | Name | Visual | Bonus/Session |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Building | Small ember | +3 XP |
| 2 | Rolling | Flickering flame | +6 XP |
| 3 | Flowing | Steady fire | +9 XP |
| 4 | Surging | Bright blaze | +12 XP |
| 5 | Unstoppable | Full bonfire | +15 XP |

**Rules:**
- Complete 1+ focus session in a day -> momentum tier goes up by 1 (max 5)
- Miss 1 day -> stays the same (grace period!)
- Miss 2 days -> drops 1 tier
- Miss 3+ days -> drops to tier 1 (never 0)
- There is no tier 0. You always have *some* momentum. Minimum state is "Building."

**Visual:** A small flame icon near the timer that grows/shrinks with tier. Always present, always warm. Never extinguished.

**Language:** "Your momentum is surging" not "Streak: 4 days." "Momentum cooling — come back to keep it warm" not "Streak broken."

**Why this works for ADHD:**
- No binary fail state -> no shame spiral
- Grace period (1 missed day) acknowledges ADHD inconsistency without punishment
- Slow decay instead of reset respects that ADHD has variable-energy days
- Always-positive framing ("building" not "zero")
- The flame visual ties into the level metaphor (Spark -> Inferno)

---

### D. Milestones (New — One-Time Achievements)

Specific achievements that unlock once and award bonus XP. Some are **visible** (goals to work toward — competence), some are **hidden** (surprise dopamine when discovered — novelty).

**Visible Milestones:**

| Milestone | Condition | XP | Icon |
|---|---|---|---|
| First Spark | Complete first focus session | 50 | sparkle |
| Hour of Power | 60 cumulative focus minutes | 75 | bolt |
| Centurion | 100 cumulative focus minutes | 100 | shield |
| Deep Diver | Complete a 50+ min session | 75 | water.waves |
| Runway Cleared | Complete a full runway | 100 | airplane.departure |
| Five-a-Day | 5 focus sessions in one day | 100 | star |

**Hidden Milestones (revealed on unlock):**

| Milestone | Condition | XP |
|---|---|---|
| Night Owl | Session completed after 10pm | 50 |
| Early Bird | Session completed before 7am | 50 |
| Triple Play | 3 sessions in one day | 50 |
| Comeback | Return after 5+ days away | 75 |
| The Marathon | 500 cumulative minutes | 150 |

**Why this works for ADHD:** Visible milestones provide concrete goals (fights amotivation). Hidden milestones provide surprise rewards (novelty-seeking satisfaction). One-time unlocks feel special — no habituation. "Comeback" milestone specifically rewards returning after absence instead of shaming it.

---

### E. Reward Card UI (The Celebration Moment)

This is the most critical piece. It replaces the current silent `totalXP += focusSessionXP` with a visible, animated, haptic moment.

**Flow:**
1. Timer hits 0 -> heavy haptic
2. **Reward Card** slides up (replaces current direct-to-mood-prompt)
3. Card shows session summary + XP earned line by line:
   - "Focus Session — +25 XP" (appears with tick animation)
   - "Flow State! — +10 XP" (if earned, with flash)
   - "Critical Focus!! — x2!" (if rolled, with shake + haptic burst)
   - "Momentum (Tier 3) — +9 XP" (with flame icon)
   - Divider
   - "Total: +44 XP" (big number, counting up)
4. If level up -> special animation + "Level Up! You're now a Flame"
5. If milestone unlocked -> badge reveal animation
6. "Continue" button -> transitions to mood prompt (existing)

**Duration:** 3-4 seconds auto-advance with option to tap through. Not blocking, not annoying — celebratory.

---

### F. Data Architecture

**New AppStorage keys:**
- `momentumTier: Int` (1-5, default 1)
- `lastSessionDate: String` (ISO date string for momentum calc)
- `unlockedMilestones: String` (comma-separated milestone IDs)
- `totalFocusMinutes: Int` (cumulative, for milestone tracking)
- `totalSessions: Int` (cumulative, for milestone tracking)

**Removed/Modified:**
- `totalXP` — kept, but now fed through the level calculation
- `sessionCount` — kept for Pomodoro cycling, separate from `totalSessions`
- `focusStreak` on TaskItem — **repurposed**: still tracked for data but UI label changes from "streak" to "momentum" and the flame icon uses the global momentum tier instead

**New files:**
- `RewardSystem.swift` — Level calculations, milestone definitions, momentum logic, variable bonus rolls, XP economy constants. Pure logic, no UI.
- `RewardCardView.swift` — The animated post-session reward card UI.

**Modified files:**
- `FocusTimer.swift` — `completeSession()` refactored to route through RewardSystem, show RewardCard before mood prompt
- `Models.swift` — TaskItem.focusStreak remains but display changes
- `ContentView.swift` — Possibly add subtle level indicator

---

### G. What Gets Removed

- The concept of "streak" as a UI-facing term (data field stays for internal tracking)
- Silent XP addition with no feedback
- The fixed `focusSessionXP = 25` constant as the sole reward
- Direct `totalXP += X` calls scattered through FocusTimer (centralized to RewardSystem)

## Part 4: Implementation Order

1. **RewardSystem.swift** — All logic: level calc, milestone defs, momentum rules, bonus rolls, XP economy
2. **RewardCardView.swift** — Animated celebration UI
3. **FocusTimer.swift** — Wire `completeSession()` through RewardSystem -> show RewardCard -> then mood prompt
4. **Momentum system** — Replace streak display, add flame visual, decay logic on app open
5. **Milestone tracking** — Check conditions in `completeSession()`, store unlocks
6. **Break rewards** — Award XP for short/long break completion (currently only focus sessions reward)
7. **Level display** — Subtle progress bar on Focus tab idle state, level in settings
8. **Milestone gallery** — A view to see unlocked + locked milestones (optional, lower priority)

## Part 5: Open Design Questions

1. **Cosmetic unlocks?** Should leveling up unlock things like new wave colors, timer themes, or celebration styles? Or keep it purely XP/level/milestones as the reward itself?

2. **Sound effects?** The app currently has no audio — only haptics. Should the Reward Card have subtle sound effects (coin sounds, level-up chime)? Or stay haptics-only?

3. **Level display location?** Options:
   - (a) Small progress bar below the timer when idle
   - (b) In a dedicated "Profile/Stats" section accessible from settings
   - (c) Both

4. **Milestone gallery priority?** Should a viewable milestone/badge collection screen be built now, or defer it and just show milestone unlocks in the Reward Card for now?

5. **Break rewards visible?** When a break timer completes, should it also show a (smaller) Reward Card? Or just silently add the 5/10 XP and note it in the next focus session's card?

## Part 6: XP UI Design Specification

### Design Language

The XP UI must match Flowcus's existing visual system:
- **Font:** Inter (Light/Regular/Medium) for all text; SF Pro Rounded reserved for timer countdown only
- **Accent:** `.cardinalRed` (`Color(red: 0.768, green: 0.118, blue: 0.227)`)
- **Material:** `.ultraThinMaterial` for floating elements (matches FloatingTabBar)
- **Motion:** `.spring(response:dampingFraction:)` for all animations (matches existing patterns)
- **Feedback:** `Haptics.impact()` and `Haptics.success()` (existing SharedComponents)
- **Dark mode:** Forced `.dark` during active sessions; respects system setting otherwise

### Design Inspirations (Research-Backed)

| App | What to take | What to skip |
|---|---|---|
| **Duolingo** | Line-item XP reveal with counting animation; celebration micro-animations; progress bar toward daily goal | Streak shame mechanics; mascot characters; social leaderboards |
| **Forest** | Simplicity — one metaphor (trees), minimal UI, focus on the timer itself; coins as tangible reward | Forest-specific visual — Flowcus uses fire/wave metaphor instead |
| **Habitica** | RPG-level XP/level system with clear progression; milestone badges | Pixel art style; overwhelming complexity; too many systems at once |
| **Nike Run Club** | Post-activity summary card with stats; clean dark UI; celebratory "achievement unlocked" moments | Social sharing focus; external platform dependencies |

### Screen 1: XP Level Bar (Focus Tab — Idle State)

**Location:** Below the mode picker, above the "Start Focus" button. Only visible when timer is idle (not running, not paused).

**Layout:**
```
+--------------------------------------------------+
|                                                    |
|                    25:00                            |  <- Timer (existing)
|                                                    |
|           [ Focus | Short | Long ]                 |  <- Mode picker (existing)
|                                                    |
|     [flame icon]  Lv 3 — Flame                     |  <- Level label
|     [=========>-----------] 142/200 XP              |  <- Progress bar
|                                                    |
|          [    Start Focus    ]                      |  <- Start button (existing)
|                                                    |
+--------------------------------------------------+
```

**Visual Details:**
- **Level label:** Inter-Medium 15pt (`.appSubhead` weight bumped to medium). Format: "Lv {n} — {name}"
- **Flame icon:** SF Symbol `flame.fill`, tinted with a gradient from `.orange` to `.cardinalRed`, size scales with momentum tier (12pt at tier 1, up to 18pt at tier 5). Subtle pulse animation at tier 4-5.
- **Progress bar:**
  - Container: `Capsule()` with `.systemGray5` fill, height 6pt, full width minus 80pt horizontal padding
  - Fill: `Capsule()` with `LinearGradient` from `.cardinalRed` to `.orange`, animated width with `.spring(response: 0.6, dampingFraction: 0.8)`
  - Corner radius: fully rounded (Capsule handles this)
- **XP label:** Inter-Light 12pt (`.appCaption`), secondary color, right-aligned under the bar. Format: "{current}/{needed} XP"
- **Animation on XP gain:** Bar fills smoothly with spring animation. If level-up occurs, the bar flashes white briefly then refills from 0.
- **Opacity:** 0.8 when idle, fades out completely during session (matches how mode picker already disappears)
- **Transition:** `.opacity.combined(with: .move(edge: .bottom))` — same pattern used by existing controls

**Color behavior:**
- Light mode: bar background `.systemGray5`, text `.secondary`
- Dark mode (system or forced): bar background `.systemGray4.opacity(0.3)`, text `.secondary`

### Screen 2: Reward Card (Post-Session Celebration)

**Trigger:** Replaces the current direct jump to mood prompt. After `completeSession()` fires, the Reward Card shows FIRST, then "Continue" leads to the existing `SessionMoodPromptView`.

**Presentation:** `.sheet` with `.presentationDetents([.height(420)])` and `.presentationDragIndicator(.visible)`. NOT a full-screen cover — keeps it lightweight and non-blocking. Background: default sheet material (system handles this).

**Layout:**
```
+--------------------------------------------------+
|              [drag indicator]                      |
|                                                    |
|              Session Complete                      |  <- Title
|           "Worked on: Task Name"                   |  <- Subtitle (if task)
|                                                    |
|  +----------------------------------------------+  |
|  |                                                |  |
|  |   Focus Session              +25 XP            |  <- Base (appears 1st)
|  |   Flow State                 +10 XP            |  <- Bonus (appears 2nd)
|  |   Critical Focus!            x2!               |  <- Random (appears 3rd)
|  |   Momentum (Flowing)          +9 XP            |  <- Momentum (appears 4th)
|  |   Runway: Small Cleared      +10 XP            |  <- Runway (if earned)
|  |                                                |  |
|  |   ─────────────────────────────                |  |
|  |   Total                      +54 XP            |  <- Total (appears last)
|  |                                                |  |
|  +----------------------------------------------+  |
|                                                    |
|     [=========>-----] Lv 3 — Flame  142/200        |  <- Level bar (animates)
|                                                    |
|             [    Continue    ]                      |  <- Goes to mood prompt
|                                                    |
+--------------------------------------------------+
```

**Visual Details:**

**Header:**
- "Session Complete" — Inter-Medium 22pt (`.appTitle2`), `.primary` color
- Subtitle — Inter-Light 15pt (`.appSubhead`), `.secondary` color
- If task was set: `"Worked on: {taskName}"`. If no task: `"{duration} minutes of focus"`

**XP Line Items:**
- Each row is an `HStack` with label left, XP right
- Label: Inter-Regular 15pt (`.appSubhead` but regular weight), `.primary`
- XP value: Inter-Medium 15pt, `.cardinalRed` (base items) or `.orange` (bonus items)
- Container: rounded rect with `.systemGray6` background (light) or `.systemGray5.opacity(0.15)` (dark), corner radius 16
- Internal padding: 16pt horizontal, 12pt vertical per row
- Rows separated by thin `.separator` divider (0.5pt)

**Line-Item Reveal Animation (the dopamine moment):**
- Each row appears sequentially with 0.3s delay between them
- Entry animation: `.opacity.combined(with: .offset(y: 8))` with `.spring(response: 0.4, dampingFraction: 0.75)`
- XP number uses `.contentTransition(.numericText())` to count up
- Haptic: `Haptics.impact(.light)` fires as each row appears
- "Critical Focus!" row (if rolled): appears with a brief `.cardinalRed` glow/flash behind the row + `Haptics.impact(.heavy)`
- Total row: slight delay after divider, bolder text (Inter-Medium 17pt), with `Haptics.success()` when it appears

**Level Progress Bar (in-card):**
- Same visual as Screen 1 level bar but embedded in the card
- Animates from pre-session value to post-session value after the total row appears
- If level-up happens: bar fills to 100%, flashes, then a "Level Up!" label appears with scale animation + `Haptics.impact(.heavy)`
- Level-up label: Inter-Medium 20pt (`.appTitle3`), `.cardinalRed`, with a subtle scale bounce from 0.8 to 1.0

**Continue Button:**
- Inter-Medium 17pt (`.appHeadline`), `.cardinalRed` text on clear background (text button, not filled — keeps focus on the reward card content)
- Padding: 16pt vertical at bottom
- Also auto-advances after 5 seconds with a subtle circular progress indicator around the button (so impatient users don't feel stuck, but the celebration has time to land)

  - Tier 1 (Building): 14pt, `.orange.opacity(0.5)`, no animation
  - Tier 2 (Rolling): 14pt, `.orange.opacity(0.7)`, no animation
  - Tier 3 (Flowing): 16pt, `.orange`, subtle slow pulse (opacity 0.8 to 1.0, 2s cycle)
  - Tier 4 (Surging): 17pt, gradient `.orange` to `.cardinalRed`, moderate pulse (1.5s cycle)
  - Tier 5 (Unstoppable): 18pt, gradient `.cardinalRed` to `.orange`, with glow shadow + fast pulse (1s cycle)
- **Label:** Inter-Light 12pt (`.appCaption`), `.secondary`, shows tier name only. No number, no "Tier X" — just the word.
- **Tap action:** None for now (future: could show momentum history)
- **Transition:** Fades out when session starts, fades back when idle

### Screen 4: Level-Up Overlay (Special Celebration)

**Trigger:** When XP earned in a session pushes the user past a level threshold. This appears ON TOP of the Reward Card as a brief overlay.

**Layout:**
```
+--------------------------------------------------+
|                                                    |
|                                                    |
|                                                    |
|                   [large flame]                    |
|                                                    |
|                   LEVEL UP                          |
|                  You're now a                       |
|                    Blaze                            |
|                                                    |
|                                                    |
|                                                    |
+--------------------------------------------------+
```

**Visual Details:**
- Full sheet overlay with `.black.opacity(0.7)` background (dims the reward card behind)
- Flame icon: SF Symbol `flame.fill`, 60pt, with gradient from `.orange` to `.cardinalRed`
- Scale animation: starts at 0.5, springs to 1.2, settles to 1.0 over 0.6s
- "LEVEL UP" — Inter-Medium 28pt (`.appTitle`), `.white`, letter-spacing slightly widened
- "You're now a" — Inter-Light 15pt (`.appSubhead`), `.white.opacity(0.7)`
- Level name — Inter-Medium 34pt (`appDisplay(size: 34)`), gradient text from `.orange` to `.cardinalRed`
- Haptic: `Haptics.impact(.heavy)` on appear, then `Haptics.success()` 0.3s later
- Auto-dismisses after 2.5 seconds, or tap to dismiss early
- Transition: fade in + scale, fade out

### Animation & Interaction Summary

| Element | Animation | Duration | Haptic |
|---|---|---|---|
| XP bar fill (idle screen) | `.spring(response: 0.6, dampingFraction: 0.8)` | ~0.6s | None |
| Reward Card appear | Sheet presentation (system default) | ~0.3s | None |
| Line item reveal | `.spring(response: 0.4, dampingFraction: 0.75)` + offset(y:8) | 0.3s each, 0.3s delay between | `.light` per row |
| Critical Focus row | Same + glow flash background | Same | `.heavy` |
| Total row reveal | Same but bolder | After 0.2s divider delay | `.success()` |
| Level bar in card | `.spring(response: 0.8, dampingFraction: 0.7)` | ~0.8s | None |
| Level-up overlay | Scale 0.5 -> 1.2 -> 1.0 | 0.6s spring | `.heavy` then `.success()` |
| Momentum flame pulse | `.easeInOut` opacity 0.8-1.0 | 1-2s repeating | None |
| Continue auto-advance | Circular trim from 0 to 1 | 5s linear | None |
